The shape of the trip

Four bases, one quiet pace.

Four bases, four cities - one travel-night at Skamania Lodge in the Gorge, two nights at The Nines in downtown Portland, two nights at Hallmark Resort & Spa on Cannon Beach, then two nights in Seattle. The route threads the Columbia Gorge, Oregon's lavender country, Portland's museums and bookstores, and the iconic Cannon Beach coast.

Where we sleep

Four hotels, all with the view.

Four properties for seven nights. Skamania Lodge for the Gorge night, two nights at The Nines in downtown Portland, two nights at the oceanfront Hallmark Resort & Spa on Cannon Beach, then two final nights in Seattle (still TBD).

Stevenson, WA · 1 night
Skamania Lodge
254-room cedar-and-stone lodge on a forested bluff above the Columbia River, set on 175 acres just east of the Bridge of the Gods. Massive timber-framed Great Hall with 60-foot stone fireplace, full-service spa, golf course, hot tub, zipline + treehouses on property. Cascade Dining Room serves PNW-forward dinner with floor-to-ceiling gorge windows.
Portland · 2 nights
The Nines (Luxury Collection)
Marriott boutique inside the historic 1909 Meier & Frank building - top eight floors of a downtown landmark with a soaring central atrium, oversized rooms, Pendleton-blanket PNW touches. Walk to Powell's, Pioneer Square, food carts, the Tom McCall waterfront. Two acclaimed in-house restaurants - Departure (8th-floor rooftop, Asian fusion, panoramic Mt. Hood view) and Urban Farmer (5th floor steakhouse).
Cannon Beach · 2 nights
Hallmark Resort & Spa
Mid-town Cannon Beach oceanfront resort - 132 rooms perched on the bluff above the sand, looking straight at Haystack Rock. Full-service spa with massage, body treatments, and ocean-view treatment rooms. Heated indoor pool, hot tub, sauna. Walking distance to dinner downtown. Most rooms have private balconies and gas fireplaces.
Seattle · 2 nights
Inn at the Market - Mountain View
76-room boutique built into Pike Place Market - the rooftop deck (free, open to all guests) gives you the postcard Elliott Bay view without paying for it. Walking distance to everything Day 7-8, plus the early Sunday airport run is just a short Uber/drive. Edgewater Hotel over-water on Pier 67 is a great alternate.

Pacific Northwest in July

What to pack.

Mid-July on the Oregon coast averages mid-60s F daytime with morning fog and low 50s overnight. Inland Hood River runs 80s, Portland mid-80s, Seattle mid-70s. Layering is the whole game. Days are long - sunsets after 9 PM.

Clothing

  • Lightweight rain shell (mid-July is mostly dry but the coast can surprise you)
  • Fleece or wool mid-layer
  • Long pants for hiking + jeans for evenings
  • Shorts for Hood River + dunes
  • Quick-dry trail pants (wet sand + rainforest mud)
  • Two t-shirts + two long-sleeves merino
  • One nice dinner outfit per person (esp. for Seattle)
  • Beach towel + swimsuit (lodges have hot tubs)

Footwear + gear

  • Trail runners or light hikers with grip - mossy rocks at tidepools
  • Sandals or slip-ons for the beach
  • Daypack (15-20L) for hikes
  • Headlamp (for Cannon Beach beach fires)
  • Binoculars (8x42 ideal) - tufted puffins nest at Haystack Rock in mid-July
  • Polarized sunglasses (cuts glare on wet sand)
  • Reusable water bottles + 2 thermoses

Don't forget

  • Pre-paid Allegiant baggage tags
  • Cash for u-cut lavender bouquets at Hope Ranch Lavender Farm
  • Washington Discover Pass (optional, day-use fee if you stop at any WA state park)
  • Sunscreen + lip balm with SPF
  • Anti-nausea meds (windy coast roads, Astoria-Megler bridge)
  • Phone car mount + Spotify offline playlists for the long Day 6 drive
  • Tide chart app (Tide Charts or similar)
  • Mosquito spray for the rainforest day
